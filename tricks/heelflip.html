Landing these tricks will help you a lot with learning this one, but they are not technically required.

Trick Tips

Here are some general tips for learning this trick.

Foot Position & Weight Distribution

Everyone has their own foot positioning and weight distribution on their tricks, so these are just some general guidelines that people tend to follow.

Put your front foot between the center of the board and the front bolts angled towards the nose.Put your back foot on the center of the tail, maybe slightly closer to the toeside edge. Keep your weight on the balls of your feet. Keep your weight directly above the center of your board and between your feet.

Forming the Trick

Everyone has their own way to form the trick, so these are just some general guidelines that people tend to follow.

In one motion, jump off your back foot, pop your tail against the ground, and lift your back knee towards your chest. In the same motion, bend your front foot at the ankle, dragging it diagonally on the griptape towards the heelside edge of your nose while also elevating your foot. When your front foot has reached the edge of the board, flick your foot upward and forward off the edge of the board. After you've flicked off the side of the board, lift your front knee towards your chest. Wait for the board to complete its 360 degree rotation before allowing your legs to come down. Try to catch the board with your back foot as you come down and land with your feet above the bolts.

Common Mistakes

  • Not getting the full flip
    • You might be flicking too early and stopping the board's flip with your front foot. Try flicking later.
    • You might not be flicking hard enough. Try flicking with more strength.
    • You might be flicking too late and stopping the board's flip with your front foot. Try flicking earlier.
  • Having the board rotate
    • You might be flicking too early. Try flicking later.
    • You might be turning your shoulders. Try to keep them parallel with the board. Holding onto a rail or fence might help you.
  • Landing on the ground before the board
    • You are not staying above the board. Try jumping higher or pulling your legs up to your chest faster and hold them there for longer.
  • Landing too far forward on the nose
    • Your weight is too far toward the nose. Make sure your weight is centered above the board before you pop and that you are not jumping too far toward the nose.
  • Landing too far back on the tail
    • Your weight is too far toward the tail. Make sure your weight is centered above the board before you pop and that you are not jumping too far toward the tail.
  • The board is rocketed
    • Your weight might be too far toward the tail. Make sure your weight is centered above the board before you pop and that you are not jumping too far toward the tail.
    • You might be flicking too early and stopping the board's flip with your front foot. Try flicking later.

Practice Tips

  • Stand on your tail with your popping foot, and practice the flicking motion with your flicking foot.
  • Pop off the ground, and flick the board with your flicking foot, but only land with the flicking foot on the board.
  • Pop off the ground, and flick the board with your flicking foot, but only land with the popping foot on the board.
  • Try the trick while you are holding onto a rail to give yourself some extra air time.
  • Pop off the ground, and flick the board with your flicking foot, but try to land on the ground after your board hits the ground.
  • Pop off the ground, and flick the board with your flicking foot, but try to land on the board upside down.
